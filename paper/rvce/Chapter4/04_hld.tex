\chapter{High Level Design}

The High Level Design (HLD) phase provides a structured representation of the overall architecture and design considerations of the proposed system. It defines the major system components, their interactions, development methodologies, and architectural strategies adopted during implementation. This chapter presents the design considerations, programming approaches, system architecture, and data flow diagrams that collectively describe the operational workflow of the project. The chapter also highlights future enhancements and concludes with a summary of the overall design framework.
